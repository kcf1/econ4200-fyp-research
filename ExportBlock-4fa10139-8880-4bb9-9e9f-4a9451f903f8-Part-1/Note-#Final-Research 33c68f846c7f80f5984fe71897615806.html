Hong Kong household consumption pattern between 1999-2019

Macro

  • Private consumption share increased from 60-70% between 1999-2019
  • Higher than East Asia and World, and diverged since 2006
  • Behaved like high / middle income countries, but trended up like low income countries since 2006, yet still lower
  • Hypothesis?
    • Engel’s Law - As income rises, the proportion of income spent on food and basic necessities declines, while the proportion spent on discretionary goods, leisure, and services increases.
    • Wealth Effect

Household

  • Micro data actually shows income trended up, while expenditure declined a bit, inconsistent to the macro data
  • But expenditure still trended up in general
  • Median income lower than then average expenditure, might be an error during C&SD data aggregation, but believe trend still reliable
  • Increase in expenditure is driven by necessities, primarily housing and food, contributed 22% and 11% out of 38% increased
  • Miscellaneous services and durable goods also contributed 4% and 1%
  • Others changes in consumption not significant

By housing type

  • Clearer pattern emerged analysed by housing type, the expenditure in share of income increased monotonically
  • As expected the divergence between different housing type is explained by the housing expenditure
  • The increase in housing expenditure are also only shown in subsidised and private households
  • Controlled for housing, only food still increased significantly, other expenditure did not show significant changes
  • The pattern in real terms in consistent with above results
  • Housing in real consumption also increased along time, increased 6% and 8% for subsidised and private household
  • Controlled for housing, decreased real consumption in food also affected public household by the most, decreased by 7%
